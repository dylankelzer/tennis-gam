# Court of Chaos - Character Model Bible

These are model briefs for the nine playable classes.

Use them as concept and 3D modeling targets, not as exact portrait copies. The goal is to capture the on-court aura, movement language, and signature energy of real tennis players, then fuse that with a grounded high-fantasy monster lineage.

## Shared visual direction

- Tone: grounded high-fantasy CRPG, layered gear, realistic materials, lived-in surfaces, and combat-ready silhouettes.
- Racquets: every racquet should feel like a signature weapon, not a modern sports prop with no fantasy integration.
- Body language: tennis movement still matters. Split-steps, shoulder rotation, recovery posture, and footwork should read clearly in every rig.
- Materials: leather, cloth, metal, carved wood, bone, enamel, feathers, scales, and runic strings.
- Modeling deliverables: front, side, back turnaround; neutral pose; serve pose; rally pose; victory pose.

## Novice - The Greenhorn Satyr

- Real-life inspiration: Coco Gauff
- Fantasy lineage: Fey satyr academy hopeful
- Silhouette: light, springy, youthful, racquet slightly too large for the body
- Outfit: training jacket, beginner armor, grip tape pouch, glowing practice balls
- Monster traits: small antlers, satyr legs, bright fey eyes
- Animation notes: bouncy idle, eager split-step, quick recovery hops
- Model prompt: 3D character model turnaround of a tennis prodigy inspired by Coco Gauff, fused with a young satyr from a high-fantasy RPG world. Athletic teenage silhouette, light leather training gear, small antlers, ash-wood racquet with runic strings, grounded realistic materials, front side back views, neutral pose.
